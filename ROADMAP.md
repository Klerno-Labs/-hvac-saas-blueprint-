# Roadmap

## Stage 0 — Thesis freeze
Lock ICP, wedge, metrics, exclusions, and pivot rules.

**Exit gate**
- ICP frozen
- headline frozen
- activation metric frozen
- validation scorecard frozen
- stage 1 assets scoped

## Stage 1 — Validation sprint
Run async outreach, Loom-first demo, landing page, and early trial demand test.

**Exit gate**
- outreach data collected
- objections ranked
- trial-start data collected
- at least one real signal stronger than polite interest
- clear pass/warning/fail decision made

## Stage 2 — MVP build
Build only the shortest path from signup to first payment.

**Exit gate**
- user can sign up
- add customer
- create job
- create quote
- complete proof of work
- send invoice
- connect payments
- collect payment
- analytics events fire correctly

## Stage 3 — Activation + retention
Improve first-payment activation and weekly reuse.

**Exit gate**
- first-payment funnel measured
- drop-off points documented
- weekly habit feature shipped
- retention baseline established

## Stage 4 — Minimum marketable product
Strengthen trust, onboarding, self-serve conversion, and proof.

**Exit gate**
- homepage proof stack live
- pricing page finalized
- onboarding improved
- docs/help content live

## Stage 5 — Payments expansion
Increase payment adoption, margin visibility, and embedded payment UX depth.

**Exit gate**
- payment adoption threshold reached
- ACH readiness evaluated
- dispute and payout monitoring in place

## Stage 6 — Collections automation
Add aging views, recovery workflows, reminder rules, and cash-control automation.

**Exit gate**
- collections module live
- invoice aging metrics tracked
- recovery lift measured

## Stage 7 — Adjacency expansion
Choose next product: memberships, financing, payroll, cards, or insurance.

**Exit gate**
- adjacency decision memo completed
- clear data-backed selection made
- risk and ops costs approved

## Stage 8 — Platformization
Expand into adjacent trades or deeper financial operating system layers.

**Exit gate**
- adjacency economics proven
- repeatable multi-vertical or platform model defined
